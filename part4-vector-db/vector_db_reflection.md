## Vector DB Use Case
A traditional keyword-based database search would not be sufficient for this use case. Keyword search relies on exact or partial word matches, which limits its ability to understand the intent or context behind a user’s query. In legal documents, important clauses such as termination conditions may be described using varied and complex language (e.g., “agreement may be dissolved under conditions…” instead of explicitly stating “termination clause”). A keyword search for “termination” might miss these relevant sections or return too many irrelevant matches, reducing both recall and precision.

In contrast, a vector database enables semantic search by leveraging embeddings. The 500-page contracts can be broken into smaller chunks (e.g., paragraphs), and each chunk is converted into a numerical vector representation using language models. These embeddings capture the meaning and context of the text rather than just the words. When a lawyer asks a question in plain English, the query is also converted into an embedding and compared against stored vectors using similarity measures like cosine similarity.

This allows the system to retrieve the most semantically relevant sections, even if they do not contain the exact keywords from the query. As a result, lawyers can quickly locate relevant clauses with higher accuracy and efficiency. Therefore, a vector database plays a critical role in enabling intelligent, context-aware search, making it far more suitable than traditional keyword-based approaches for analyzing large and complex legal documents.
